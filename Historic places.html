Historical Places in India

Amaravati Stupa (Amaravati, Andhra Pradesh)

Ancient Buddhist stupa and important early historic Buddhist site (1st century BCE–3rd century CE).

Tawang Monastery (Tawang, Arunachal Pradesh)

Largest Buddhist monastery in India (17th century) and a major center of Tibetan Buddhism.

Kamakhya Temple (Guwahati, Assam)

One of India’s oldest Shakti shrines, a major Hindu pilgrimage center (Tantric traditions).

Mahabodhi Temple (Bodh Gaya, Bihar)

Where Gautama Buddha attained enlightenment , UNESCO World Heritage Buddhist complex.

Bhoramdeo Temple (Kawardha, Chhattisgarh)

11th-century Hindu (Shiva) temple complex with detailed stone carvings.

Shri Mangeshi Temple (Priol, Goa)

Major Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva (well known for its architecture and festivals).

Sun Temple (Modhera, Gujarat)

11th-century Hindu temple dedicated to the Sun God (famous stepwell & carvings).

Brahma Sarovar (Thanesar / Kurukshetra, Haryana)

Venerated Hindu tank and pilgrimage site linked to Kurukshetra’s Mahabharata tradition.

Hidimba Devi Temple (Manali, Himachal Pradesh)

Iconic wooden Hindu temple (16th century) set in cedar forest near Manali.

Jagannath Temple (Ranchi, Jharkhand)

Important regional Hindu temple dedicated to Jagannath; hosts annual Rath Yatra.

Virupaksha Temple (Hampi, Karnataka)

Active Hindu temple at Hampi — part of the Vijayanagara ruins and UNESCO world heritage area.

Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple (Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala)

Historic and richly ornamented Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu (Anantha-sayana form).

Sanchi Stupa (Sanchi, Madhya Pradesh)

Ancient Buddhist stupa complex commissioned by Emperor Ashoka (3rd century BCE onwards).

Ajanta Caves (Aurangabad, Maharashtra)

2nd century BCE–6th century CE Buddhist cave monuments — murals and rock-cut architecture.

Shree Govindajee Temple (Imphal, Manipur)

Historic Vaishnavite Hindu temple in Imphal, central to Manipuri religious life.

Nartiang Monoliths (Nartiang, Meghalaya)

Historic Jaintia kingdom monuments — large monoliths and ritual sites. (regionally important sacred structures)

Kamalanagar Buddhist Temple (Mizoram)

A notable Buddhist shrine serving local communities — representing Buddhist presence in parts of the state.

Historic Sacred Site (Nagaland*)

Modern Buddhist monastery serving the local Buddhist community, with typical Tibetan-style architecture.

Lingaraja Temple (Bhubaneswar, Odisha)

11th-century temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, a major example of Odishan temple architecture.

Durgiana Temple (Amritsar, Punjab)

Major Hindu temple in Amritsar, architecturally similar to the nearby Golden Temple but dedicated to Durga/Lakshmi.

Brahma Temple (Pushkar, Rajasthan)

One of the very few temples dedicated to Lord Brahma; an important Hindu pilgrimage site at Pushkar.

Rumtek Monastery (Rumtek, Sikkim)

Prominent Tibetan Buddhist monastery and seat for the Kagyu lineage in Sikkim.

Arunachalesvara Temple (Tiruvannamalai, Tamil Nadu)

Major Shaivaite temple and one of the Pancha Bhoota Sthalams (fire element).

Phanigiri Buddhist Site (Phanigiri, Telangana)

An important early historic Buddhist monastic complex with stupas and rock-cut remains.

Unakoti (Kailashahar area, Tripura)

Ancient rock-cut Hindu sculptures (Shiva and others) — an important pilgrimage and archaeological site.

Sarnath (Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh)

Site where Buddha taught his first sermon — Dhamek Stupa and the Sarnath Archaeological complex.

Badrinath Temple (Badrinath, Uttarakhand)

One of the Char Dham shrines — ancient and major Hindu pilgrimage site in the Himalaya.

Mayapur (Mayapur, West Bengal)

Major Vaishnavite pilgrimage center associated with Sri Chaitanya Mahaprabhu.

Cellular Jail (Port Blair, Andaman & Nicobar Islands)

Historic colonial-era prison used during British rule; a major landmark in Port Blair. While not a religious site, it is an iconic heritage monument of the islands.

Historic Sacred Sites & Shrines (Chandigarh)*

Chandigarh is primarily a modern planned city. Nearby historic Hindu temples (e.g., Mata Mansa Devi regionally) serve pilgrim needs—Rock Garden shown as city landmark.

Lotus Temple (New Delhi, Delhi)

Bahá'í House of Worship (architectural landmark).

Arulmigu Manakula Vinayagar Temple (Puducherry)

Historic Hindu temple in Puducherry dedicated to Lord Ganesha, famous for its colorful architecture and vibrant festivals.

Leh Palace / Buddhist Monasteries (Leh, Ladakh)

Leh Palace & many Tibetan Buddhist monasteries (e.g., Thiksey, Hemis) — major Buddhist heritage in Ladakh.
