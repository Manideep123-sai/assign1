Historical Places in India

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum in Agra, built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar is a 73-meter tall minaret in Delhi, built by Qutb-ud-din Aibak in 1193. It is the tallest brick minaret in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Agra Fort

Agra Fort is a historical fort in Agra, built by Emperor Akbar in 1565. It is made of red sandstone and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Hampi

Hampi is a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Karnataka, known for its ruins of the Vijayanagara Empire. It is famous for its temples and architecture.

Konark Sun Temple

The Konark Sun Temple in Odisha is a 13th-century temple dedicated to the Sun God. It is designed in the shape of a chariot and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Ajanta Caves

The Ajanta Caves in Maharashtra are rock-cut Buddhist cave monuments dating back to the 2nd century BCE. They are renowned for their paintings and sculptures.
